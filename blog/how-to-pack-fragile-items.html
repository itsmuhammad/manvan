How to Pack Fragile Items Safely

Protect dishes, glass, and artwork with these packing techniques. Bubble wrap, boxes, and pro tips.

Fragile items are among the most commonly damaged during a move. With the right materials and techniques, you can pack dishes, glassware, mirrors, and artwork so they arrive in one piece. Here's how.

1. Use the Right Materials

Stock up on: bubble wrap, packing paper, sturdy boxes (dish packs or mirror boxes), and packing tape. Avoid newspaper for fine china—the ink can transfer. Use clean packing paper or tissue instead.

2. Wrap Each Item Individually

Don't stack plates or bowls without wrapping. Wrap each piece in packing paper or bubble wrap, then place plates vertically in the box (never flat). Fill empty spaces with crumpled paper so nothing shifts.

3. Label Boxes Clearly

Mark boxes "FRAGILE" and "This Side Up." Place heavier items at the bottom and lighter ones on top. Keep fragile boxes separate from heavier furniture during loading.

4. Mirrors and Artwork

Use specialized mirror or picture boxes. Wrap in bubble wrap first, then secure in the box with foam corners. Never lean mirrors flat—they can crack under pressure.

What is the best way to pack fragile items for moving?

Avoid newspaper for fine china — the ink can transfer and stain. Use clean packing paper or tissue paper instead.
